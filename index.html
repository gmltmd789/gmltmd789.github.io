Building more human-like, real-time voice and omni-modal agents.

Email CV Google Scholar GitHub LinkedIn SIA Lab

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Artificial Intelligence at the University of Seoul (since March 2026), where I lead the Speech & Interactive AI Lab (SIA Lab). My current research centers on full-duplex speech LLMs, spoken dialogue models that listen and speak simultaneously in real time, and on extending them into omni-modal agents: supporting egocentric visual understanding for smart glasses and talking faces, multi-turn tool calling, and diffusion-based LLM architectures.

Previously, I was a Senior Research Engineer at Qualcomm AI Research Korea (until February 2026), where I worked on training full-duplex speech LLMs. I received my Ph.D. from the Data Science & AI Lab (DSAIL) at Seoul National University (SNU), advised by Sungroh Yoon, where my research focused on speech synthesis (text-to-speech, voice conversion) and speech large language and dialog models (speech LLMs), alongside explorations of generative models in other domains (vision, NLP). I received my B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Seoul National University.

Research Interests

Four threads, one goal: voice agents that feel like talking to a person. Real-time, multimodal, and able to act.

Core Topic

Full-Duplex Speech LLMs

Dialogue models that listen while speaking: handling barge-ins, backchannels, and self-repairs for natural, real-time conversation.

extending toward ↓

Omni-Modal Agents

Egocentric video understanding for smart-glasses assistants and talking-face generation.

Multi-Turn Tool Calling

Voice agents that search, book, and act across turns while the conversation keeps flowing.

Diffusion LLM Architectures

Reshaping autoregressive speech LLMs into diffusion LMs for parallel generation and lower latency.
